Linus Torvalds

The man who created linux

Here’s a time line of Linus Torvalds life:

  • 1969-Born in Helsinki, Finland.

  • 1988-Started attending the university of Helsinki.

  • 1989-Joined the finish army brigade as part of his mandatory military service.

  • 1990-Rejoined the university of Helsinki.

  • 1991-Purchased an Intel 80386 to start work on Linux.

  • 1991-First public release of Linux prototype.

  • 1994-Release of Linux version 1.0.

  • 1995-Successfully ran Linux on AlphaStation.

  • 1996-Had the 9793 Torvalds Asteroid named after him.

  • 1997-Received his masters degree from the department of computer science at the university of Helsinki.

  • 1997-Started work at Transmeta.

  • 1998-Received EFF Pioneer Award.

  • 1999-Named by MIT technology Review TR100 as one of the world’s top 100 innovators under age 35.

  • 2000-Awarded the Lovelace Medal from the British Computer Society.

  • 2001-shared the Takeda Award for Social/Economic Well-Being with Richard Stallman and Ken Sakamura.

  • 2003-the naming of the asteroid moon Linus was motivated in part by the fact that the discoverer was an enthusiastic Linux user.

  • 2005-Received the Vollum Award from Reed College.

  • 2008-Inducted into the hall of the fellows of Computer History Museum in Mountain View, California.

  • 2012-was declared one of two winners of that year's Millennium Technology Prize, along with Shinya Yamanaka.

  • 2018-Ibuka award was conferred to Linus Torvalds "For his leadership of the development and proliferation of Linux.".
